# The Chimera Secured Pilot Program

## How The Pilot Works

The Chimera Secured pilot program is a structured engagement where an MSP deploys the system for a subset of users in a customer tenant and validates detection accuracy with real-world data. The pilot is designed to prove value quickly — an MSP can go from zero to a running deployment with enrolled users in about 30 minutes.

The pilot proceeds through three phases. Shadow mode is the initial phase where CPA scores every outbound email and logs the verdicts without taking any action. This phase establishes baseline accuracy and builds confidence in the system's ability to correctly distinguish between the real account owner and potential impostors. Warn mode is the second phase where administrators are notified when CPA flags a suspicious email, but the email is still delivered. This phase validates that the alerts are actionable and the false positive rate is acceptable. Enforce mode is the final phase where CPA can quarantine suspicious outbound emails before they leave the organization. This phase is only activated after the MSP and customer are confident in the system's accuracy.

## Pilot Success Criteria

A successful pilot demonstrates that CPA can correctly identify the account owner's writing with 80 percent or higher accuracy across enrolled users. It shows a false positive rate low enough that enforce mode would not disrupt normal business operations. And it validates the deployment model — that an MSP can deploy, configure, and manage the system without Chimera Secured engineering support.

## Pilot Timeline

The typical pilot runs two to four weeks. Week one covers deployment, Azure AD configuration, enrollment of the initial user cohort, and shadow-mode data collection. Week two continues shadow-mode observation with accuracy review and any needed threshold tuning. Weeks three and four transition to warn mode, where administrators actively review alerts and provide feedback. The MSP can begin discussing enforce-mode transition with the customer once the warn-mode data validates accuracy.

## What MSPs Get From The Pilot

MSPs gain a new revenue stream and a competitive differentiator. Chimera Secured addresses a threat class — authenticated account takeover — that no other tool in their current security stack covers. This gives them a unique offering for customers who already have endpoint protection, email filtering, and identity management but remain vulnerable to credential-based attacks.

The pilot also demonstrates the voice profile capability, which MSPs can position as a productivity tool alongside the security product. Being able to generate accurate writing style guides for key personnel is valuable for executive communications, marketing, and organizational knowledge management.

## Partner Structure

Chimera Secured's initial go-to-market is through MSP partnerships. The first partner, Rain Networks, has relationships covering up to 60,000 seats across their downstream partners, with actual pilot size determined by which partners opt in. A second MSP partner is ready to engage once the product is validated through the initial pilot.

MSPs deploy and manage Chimera Secured on behalf of their customers. The MSP handles the Azure AD setup, user enrollment, ongoing monitoring, and escalation handling. Chimera Secured provides the technology, documentation, and technical support to make the MSP successful.

## Pricing Context

Chimera Secured is positioned as a premium security layer that addresses the most expensive category of email-based attacks. Business Email Compromise driven by account takeover represents the largest single category of cybercrime losses reported to the FBI. The value proposition is straightforward: every other tool checks if the email came from the right server; Chimera Secured checks if it came from the right person. The price point reflects this unique defensive capability and the fact that no competing product addresses the same threat surface.
