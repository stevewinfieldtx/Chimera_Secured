# Voice Profiles — Exportable Writing Style Guides

## What Voice Profiles Are

Chimera Secured includes a Voice Profile feature that translates each user's behavioral fingerprint into a natural-language writing style guide. The voice profile is a 10-section document that describes exactly how someone writes — their vocabulary habits, sentence structure preferences, punctuation patterns, tone characteristics, and unique quirks. This profile can be copied and pasted into any large language model like ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude as a system instruction, and that LLM will write in the user's authentic voice.

## How Voice Profiles Are Generated

During enrollment, CPA computes approximately 48 aggregate writing statistics from the user's email corpus. These statistics span six categories: vocabulary metrics like type-token ratio and word complexity; grammar patterns including passive voice percentage and pronoun ratios; punctuation rates for commas, exclamation marks, questions, dashes, ellipses, semicolons, and parentheses; sentence characteristics like average length, fragment rate, and sentence variety; paragraph structure; and tone indicators including a formality score on a 0 to 100 scale.

The voice profile generator then translates these statistics into prose descriptions using threshold-based rules. For example, if someone's type-token ratio is above 0.65, the profile describes their vocabulary as notably varied and diverse. If their exclamation mark rate is above 3 per thousand words, it notes that they use exclamation marks frequently to express enthusiasm.

## What Makes Voice Profiles Valuable

Voice profiles solve a practical problem that many organizations face. When an executive or key person needs communications drafted on their behalf — by an assistant, a marketing team, or an AI tool — matching their authentic voice is critical. Without a voice profile, drafts sound generic. With a voice profile loaded into an LLM, the output matches the person's actual writing patterns at a detailed level that goes far beyond simple tone instructions like "be professional" or "sound casual."

The voice profile captures patterns the writer themselves may not consciously recognize, like their tendency to use dashes instead of semicolons, or their habit of starting emails with action-oriented sentences rather than greetings. These subtle patterns are what make writing feel authentically like a specific person versus generically competent.

## Voice Profile Sections

The voice profile contains ten sections covering the full spectrum of writing behavior. Vocabulary and Word Choice describes the person's word complexity, vocabulary diversity, and preferred jargon level. Grammar and Syntax covers sentence construction patterns, passive versus active voice tendencies, and pronoun usage. Punctuation Fingerprint details which punctuation marks the person favors and avoids. Sentence Architecture describes their typical sentence lengths and how much they vary. Rhetorical Patterns covers their persuasion style and argument construction. Paragraph Structure describes how they organize ideas into paragraphs. Tone and Formality captures their position on the formal-casual spectrum. Coherence and Flow describes their transition style between ideas. Signature Quirks highlights the most distinctive and unusual patterns in their writing. Email-Specific Patterns covers their greeting and sign-off habits, typical email length, and response style.
